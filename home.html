Solar cells convert sunlight into electricity using semiconductor materials, providing a renewable energy source for various applications, from small gadgets to large power grids.

Polymer Solar Cells

Pros:

  • Low cost: Cheaper to produce.
  • Lightweight: Easy to transport and install.
  • Versatile: Can be made flexible and transparent for various applications.
  • Eco-friendly: Less environmental impact during manufacturing.

Cons:

  • Lower efficiency: Generally less efficient than silicon-based cells.
  • Poor stability: Degrades faster with exposure to light, heat, and oxygen.
  • Shorter lifespan: Requires more frequent replacement and maintenance.
  • Commercial challenges: Not yet widely commercialized, with room for technical improvement.
